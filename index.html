Advocacy Objectives

I hope to convince you that:
  • Using computational exercises as part of physics pedagogy can give students a deeper understanding of physics and mathematics than pen-and-paper math alone
  • The barrier to entry for computational pedagogy is really not that high!
  • PICUP can help lower that barrier even further, and provide lots of support wherever you might need it!


First steps: the PICUP website

PICUP is...

  • ... a loose partnership of dozens of people across the country
  • ... committed to pluralism in teaching methods: there are many, many ways to integrate computation into teaching (and they're all correct)
  • ... explicitly platform agnostic (pedagogy is bigger than a particular set of tools)
  • ... at heart a friendly community (come join our Slack team!)
